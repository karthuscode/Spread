# Purpose

The Masked Surgeon establishes the upper boundary of mascot identity and visual confidence within *Spread*. He must embody protection without resembling a believable medical professional. His distorted face, unusual hair, crooked posture, sealed costume shapes, and obsessive equipment arrangement should communicate preparation through caricature. The result must feel iconic, psychologically strange, and impossible to confuse with an ordinary surgeon.

## Documentation Role

This document is the Masked Surgeon Character Bible and serves as the long-form human reference for creative intent and visual direction. It is not intended as direct image-generation input. Future AI image generation should use a compact Image Specification derived from this Bible together with the shared [AI Generation Guide](../ai-generation-guide.md).

# Narrative Role

The Masked Surgeon represents disciplined resistance filtered through psychological eccentricity. Intelligence and preparation remain intact, but they have developed into an almost absurd personal ritual. He is neither an action hero nor a sinister authority figure. His steadiness is strange enough to be funny: the surrounding contamination appears less unusual than the person calmly prepared to meet it.

# Emotional Impression

Players should smile first, then feel a small, intriguing discomfort. The character is charismatic, protected, and impossible to surprise, but his delight is slightly too intense and his composure slightly too practiced. The emotional read is “strange, prepared, magnetic,” never murderous, monstrous, or horrific. Unease comes from personality and exaggeration rather than threat.

# Core Personality

- Intensely observant and unpredictably calm
- Obsessively precise in eccentric personal ways
- Experienced, theatrical, and self-possessed
- Charismatic despite psychological oddness
- Patient, calculating, and visibly delighted by problems
- Dryly funny through visual contradiction
- Resistant to panic but comfortable with chaos
- Human in identity, implausible in proportion and posture

# Visual Identity

The character is an iconic adult mascot with an oversized head, compressed torso, asymmetrical shoulders, wild swept hair, and a bent, question-mark posture. A warped protective mask, crooked eyewear, tiny cap, and enormous uneven collar suggest his profession without defining him. His face dominates the design. Tiny instruments are arranged with obsessive precision against otherwise chaotic costume shapes. Every proportion should feel authored, surprising, and anatomically implausible.

# Shape Language

Use bulging curves, pinched wedges, crooked shield forms, and abrupt changes of scale. A huge hair-and-face mass sits above a narrow twisted torso. One shoulder rises sharply while the other collapses into a sloping curve. Protective shapes remain enclosed, but never orderly or symmetrical. Concentrate complexity in the face, hair, and mask; reduce everything else to large graphic masses.

# Silhouette

The silhouette relies on a huge off-center hair crest, enlarged mask-and-cheek profile, one high shoulder, one low shoulder, hidden neck, and a curved question-mark spine. The head should feel almost too large for the torso. Irregularity is intentional but controlled. As a single dark shape, the character must read as this specific mascot, not merely as a protected medical worker.

# Facial Design

Use aggressively caricatured mature proportions: oversized cheekbones, a long crescent nose pressing against the mask, narrow jaw, uneven brow, and broad graphic facial planes. The mask stretches around the face rather than hiding its construction. Asymmetry should be obvious. The character remains recognizably human, but anatomical accuracy is undesirable. Avoid conventional handsomeness and ordinary professional portrait proportions.

# Eyes

The eyes are deliberately mismatched: one large, sharply focused eye and one smaller eye compressed by amusement. Pupils are tiny but readable, with hard graphic highlights. Crooked translucent lenses distort them differently. Brows create opposing angles, suggesting simultaneous calculation and delight. Avoid dead eyes, supernatural glow, or reflections that erase the expression.

# Expression

The default expression is an unsettling but charismatic smile that visibly pulls the mask into a wide curved form. He appears delighted by a problem nobody else understands. The expression should be funny before it becomes uncomfortable: confident, conspiratorial, and strangely reassuring. Avoid rage, predatory menace, vacant madness, or realistic clinical neutrality.

# Pose

Use a crooked three-quarter pose with the torso leaning one way and the oversized head counterbalancing the other. One shoulder nearly touches the jaw. A long gloved hand may hover near the mask or arrange a tiny tool with excessive delicacy. The posture should feel peculiar even in stillness. Avoid upright professional portraiture, heroic stances, lunges, and weapon-like gestures.

# Costume

The costume combines black protective fabrics, a warped muted-teal overshirt, enormous crooked collar, long fitted gloves, uneven straps, and a tiny cap almost lost beneath wild hair. Closures follow obsessive repeated rhythms while the garment itself sits asymmetrically. Medical association comes through material and protection cues, not accurate uniform design. Avoid white coats, standard scrubs, neckties, authority uniforms, branding, and literal professional costume.

# Medical Equipment

Use one oversized curved inspection lens and a cluster of tiny mismatched instruments arranged with implausible precision. Scale contrast should make the equipment feel absurd and personal. Forms remain graphic and non-weaponized, with oxidized surfaces and chemical staining. Avoid exposed needles, presented scalpels, realistic machinery, and procedural accuracy.

# Materials

Combine black woven fabric, stained rubber, cloudy glass, waxy mask material, oxidized metal, and damp-looking protective surfaces. Chemical residue and unnatural texture may suggest contamination without bodily damage. Reflections should be crooked and selective. Materials feel aged, handled, and infected by the environment, never luxurious, sterile, bloody, or realistically decomposed.

# Color Direction

Build the palette around charcoal, black fabric, deep desaturated green, muted teal, oxidized grey, and sickly pale but living skin. Small green contamination accents and strange fluid stains introduce infection. Dark values should shape the mascot silhouette rather than create horror concealment. Avoid bright clinical white, clean cyan uniforms, blood red, gold, jewel tones, and cheerful medical colour coding.

# Lighting

Use theatrical directional lighting that carves the oversized face, hair crest, mask distortion, and crooked shoulders into graphic planes. A contaminated green reflection may rise from below while a muted rim separates black fabrics. Preserve both mismatched eyes through the lenses. Darkness should feel chemical and psychological, not like a threatening operating room.

# Rendering Style

Render polished grotesque caricature with clean graphic edges, selective unnatural texture, and strong material separation. Push facial planes, scale changes, and asymmetry beyond believable anatomy. Use broad gradients and crisp focal detail without realism-led correction. Secondary surfaces remain simplified. Downscaling must preserve the hair crest, smile-mask, mismatched eyes, and crooked shoulder rhythm.

# Art Style

Target premium mobile-game finish with comic-book exaggeration, animated dark-comedy appeal, and surreal mascot confidence. Exaggeration is foundational rather than decorative. Grotesque caricature refers to distortion and personality, never gore or bodily damage. Dark humour comes from the collision between obsessive preparedness and an impossible face. The character should feel unmistakably native to *Spread*.

# Composition

Frame the upper torso in a centered three-quarter view, allowing the crooked posture and enormous hair crest to press against controlled margins. The face occupies more area than a believable portrait would permit. Keep equipment within the distorted torso mass. Use a simple dark neutral field or contaminated tonal shape, never a literal hospital room.

# Readability

At small sizes, preserve the hair crest, question-mark posture, stretched smile-mask, mismatched eyes, and asymmetrical shoulders. Reduce instruments, stains, seams, and texture first. Maintain strong value separation between pale face, dark costume, teal mask, and green contamination. The silhouette and expression must identify the mascot before any professional detail is visible.

# Animation Ideas

- Uneven breathing that alternately lifts the high and low shoulder
- Mismatched blinking followed by an unnaturally focused stare
- Crooked lens reflection bending across each eye at a different speed
- Hair crest settling, then springing back into its impossible shape
- Long-fingered glove adjustment performed with excessive delicacy
- Smile-mask stretching wider for a moment before relaxing
- Tiny instruments vibrating into obsessive alignment
- Bent head drifting off-axis, then snapping gently back toward the viewer

# Relationship to Other Symbols

The Masked Surgeon holds the strongest hierarchy through facial identity, impossible proportions, and silhouette authority. Relative to the Nurse, he is more enclosed and psychologically commanding. Relative to Human2 and Human3, his caricature is more deliberate, protected, and iconic. Relative to VirusWild, his chaos is internal and controlled rather than explosively active. Relative to equipment symbols, he contributes the strongest personality and most memorable facial rhythm.

# Things to Avoid

- Photorealistic anatomy, skin pores, or clinical simulation
- Ordinary believable surgeons or realistic professional portraits
- Correct anatomy, balanced beauty, or conventional heroic proportions
- Anime facial construction, chibi proportions, or oversized cute eyes
- Horror lighting, corpse-like skin, or threatening shadows
- Blood, stains, wounds, gore, body horror, or infection symptoms
- Zombie cues, torn clothing, exposed teeth, or vacant eyes
- Sinister plague-doctor shapes or bird-like masks
- Generic white coat, stethoscope, clipboard, or head-mirror stereotypes
- Aggressive surgical tools, weapon poses, or exposed needles
- Gold trim, gemstones, luxury ornament, or casino iconography
- Superhero anatomy, military armour, or action-combat posing
- Excessively bulky protective suits that hide all humanity
- Comedic incompetence, panic, slapstick, or smug arrogance
- Dense micro-detail that collapses at slot-symbol scale
- Fully reflective eyewear that conceals the eyes
- Sterile corporate stock-character symmetry
- Calm neutral professionalism without psychological character
- Subtle proportions that disappear into generic human design
- Literal hospital interiors competing with the character
- Neon toxic palettes that visually merge with infection imagery

# AI Generation Notes

Future generated artwork should prioritize unmistakable mascot identity, the huge hair-and-face mass, crooked posture, stretched smile-mask, mismatched eyes, and psychologically unusual charisma. Proportions should be intentionally pushed beyond anatomy rather than corrected toward realism. Evaluate silhouette and facial memorability at thumbnail size before materials or equipment. Reject ordinary surgeons, realistic professional portraiture, generic beauty, subtle asymmetry, horror, gore, anime conventions, and luxury decoration. The result should feel surprising but coherent within the shared *Spread* universe.
