# Purpose

The Nurse expands the *Spread* cast with a second mascot built from restless care rather than controlled preparation. Her design must communicate an urgent desire to help through expression, posture, and an unmistakable top-heavy silhouette. She establishes that characters can share the same grotesque caricature, contaminated materials, and psychological dark comedy while having completely different visual rhythms.

## Documentation Role

This document is the Nurse Character Bible and serves as the long-form human reference for her creative intent and visual direction. It inherits the shared Visual Direction and Visual DNA without restating them. AI-assisted exploration should use the derived [Nurse Image Specification](nurse.image-spec.md) together with the shared [AI Generation Guide](../ai-generation-guide.md).

# Narrative Role

The Nurse represents care intensified into cheerful obsession. Helpfulness has become a permanent state of attention: she is always leaning closer, noticing one more symptom, and preparing one more unnecessary comfort. Her eccentricity is affectionate rather than malicious. The symbolic tension comes from genuine warmth expressed with slightly alarming enthusiasm.

# Emotional Impression

The first response should be amused curiosity. She feels lively, welcoming, and eager to engage, followed by the realization that her attention may be difficult to escape. She should appear eccentric rather than evil, psychologically unusual rather than dangerous, and energetic rather than frantic. The discomfort comes from excessive interest, never cruelty or threat.

# Core Personality

- Intensely curious and socially fearless
- Generous with attention, whether requested or not
- Quick, energetic, and difficult to discourage
- Cheerfully intrusive without hostile intent
- Improvisational rather than precise
- Proud of small acts of care
- Easily fascinated by unusual details
- Warm, eccentric, and slightly unpredictable

# Visual Identity

The Nurse is a compact, forward-leaning adult mascot beneath an impossibly oversized medical cap. The cap is her defining feature: a broad, off-axis crescent of folded fabric that seems much too large for her narrow head and small body. Beneath it, a lively triangular face, springy side curls, darting eyes, and a mask worn loosely under the chin create an open, overinvested expression. Her silhouette spreads horizontally at the top, then contracts through a short torso before opening into active bent elbows.

# Shape Language

Use buoyant circles and compressed ovals interrupted by quick triangular accents. The enormous cap forms one clean crescent mass. The face narrows toward a small pointed chin, while puffed cheeks and rounded sleeves keep her inviting. Elbows, brows, collar points, and stray hair introduce sharp directional energy. Avoid the Surgeon’s tall hair mass, enclosed shield shapes, and long crooked torso rhythm.

# Silhouette

The silhouette is dominated by the cap, which tilts dramatically to one side like an unstable fabric canopy. A small head and pinched torso hang beneath it, with both elbows held away from the body at different heights. The pose creates clear negative spaces between forearms and waist. At thumbnail size, the broad cap, forward head, and uneven wing-like arm rhythm must identify her without facial or costume detail.

# Facial Design

Use a short, tapered face with full asymmetrical cheeks, a tiny upturned nose, and a wide mouth compressed by accumulated enthusiasm. One cheek rises higher because she is always half-smiling. The forehead appears narrow beneath the cap, increasing the impression that the headgear is barely contained. Keep the face human-readable but deliberately unconventional; avoid prettiness, delicate fashion proportions, and ordinary portrait anatomy.

# Eyes

Both eyes are large and alert, but not identical. One is round and fixed directly on the viewer; the other angles slightly toward a nearby point of interest. Use dark, lively pupils with compact highlights and clearly different lid shapes. The gaze should suggest that she is observing the viewer and something just beyond them simultaneously. Avoid vacant mania, glowing infection effects, or cute doll-like symmetry.

# Expression

Her default expression is an eager, compressed grin with raised brows and a small intake of breath, as though she has just noticed something fascinating. The loosely lowered mask leaves the expression completely readable. Warmth must remain present even when the intensity feels excessive. Avoid the Surgeon’s knowing, controlled smile; her expression is immediate, open, and almost impossible to contain.

# Pose

Use a forward-tilted three-quarter pose with the head arriving before the torso. One shoulder rises while the opposite elbow opens outward, producing a lively zigzag. Her hands may hover near the edge of the frame as if ready to adjust, inspect, or offer something, but no action should become procedural. The pose should feel paused mid-bustle, never upright, elegant, heroic, or threatening.

# Costume

Dress her in a short, warped work tunic with uneven rounded sleeves, a narrow offset collar, mismatched fasteners, and an apron panel that sits slightly sideways. The oversized cap is a soft architectural form rather than an accurate uniform component. A protective mask hangs under the chin on unequal straps. Clothing should suggest a caring profession through shorthand while remaining invented, aged, and subordinate to her personality.

# Medical Equipment

Keep equipment minimal and compact: a cloudy thermometer tucked into one seam, a small rubber bulb, or two tiny labelled tabs with illegible markings. Items should feel frequently handled and personally organized in an improvised way. Nothing should resemble a weapon or realistic procedure kit. Avoid prominent syringes, scalpels, stethoscope stereotypes, modern hospital devices, and equipment that competes with the cap.

# Materials

The cap and tunic use thick old fabric with softened edges, warped seams, and subtle chemical discoloration. Sleeves have a slightly waxy coating; the lowered mask is dull rubberized cloth. Fasteners are oxidized metal, while any glass is cloudy and scratched. Materials should feel repeatedly cleaned but never truly clean. Contamination appears as muted stains and residue, not bodily fluids or decay.

# Color Direction

Build the palette from faded blue-green, dirty cream, charcoal, muted petrol teal, oxidized grey, and restrained yellow-green staining. The cap should be lighter than the costume but never pure white, allowing its silhouette to separate against a dark field. Warm, desaturated skin provides a friendly focal area. Avoid bright clinical blue, candy pink, pristine white, blood red, gold, and polished luxury accents.

# Lighting

Use energetic directional light that defines the cap’s folded planes, the forward face, and the uneven elbows. A soft contaminated green bounce may touch the underside of the cap and chin, while a restrained rim separates its broad edge from the background. Both eyes and the lowered mask must remain clear. Avoid ominous underlighting, operating-room spotlights, and glossy beauty lighting.

# Rendering Style

Render as polished stylized caricature with bold, controlled planes and selective texture. Preserve the disproportion between enormous cap and compact body rather than correcting balance or fabric behaviour. Concentrate facial detail around the eyes, brows, and grin; simplify seams, stains, and small equipment. The result should retain an animated elasticity while matching the cast’s premium comic-book finish.

# Art Style

The Nurse belongs to an animated psychological dark comedy, not a realistic medical setting. Her grotesque quality comes from disproportion, relentless curiosity, and social intensity rather than ugliness or damage. Shapes should feel designed for immediate recognition and expressive posing. Humour comes from the contrast between sincere care and an absurdly overcommitted visual presence.

# Composition

Frame the upper torso in a square, three-quarter composition. Leave enough margin for the cap’s broad crescent to read cleanly without touching every edge. Let the forward face occupy the center while the cap creates an off-axis top anchor and the elbows stabilize the lower frame. Use a simple dark neutral or contaminated tonal background, never a literal ward or treatment room.

# Readability

At small size, preserve the oversized tilted cap, compact forward body, wide attentive eyes, lowered mask, and uneven elbow shapes. Reduce stitching, equipment, stains, and fabric texture first. Separate the lighter cap and face from the darker tunic with strong value grouping. If the cap reads as normal-sized or the posture becomes upright, the design has lost its identity.

# Animation Ideas

- Cap settling slowly after the body has already stopped moving
- Alternating blinks while each eye tracks a different point
- Quick forward lean followed by a tiny corrective retreat
- Mask strap bouncing beneath the chin
- Uneven sleeves rising with eager breathing
- One curl escaping from under the cap and springing back
- Fingers beginning several helpful gestures before choosing one
- Brows lifting in delayed fascination

# Relationship to Other Symbols

Beside the Masked Surgeon, the Nurse shares exaggerated anatomy, asymmetry, contaminated materials, and psychologically charged expression. She contrasts through a broad horizontal cap, compact forward body, open face, and quick outward gestures. He appears enclosed, controlled, vertical, and calculating; she appears open, buoyant, horizontal, and immediately reactive. Her silhouette and facial rhythm must remain distinct even when both are shown in grayscale.

# Things to Avoid

- A realistic nurse, healthcare portrait, or believable uniform
- A female variation of the Masked Surgeon
- The Surgeon’s tall hair crest, hidden neck, crooked lenses, or question-mark spine
- Multiple competing signature exaggerations
- A normal-sized, accurate, pristine nurse cap
- Conventional beauty, glamour styling, or pin-up presentation
- Cute anime eyes, chibi anatomy, or doll-like symmetry
- Maternal saint imagery or passive bedside gentleness
- Sinister asylum stereotypes or cruel institutional authority
- Screaming, rage, predatory excitement, or vacant madness
- Blood, wounds, gore, body horror, zombies, or medical torture
- Bright hospital blues, pure clinical white, or sterile plastic
- Gold, gems, luxury trim, or casino iconography
- Prominent needles, scalpels, weaponized tools, or procedural action
- Dense accessories that weaken the cap silhouette
- Perfect posture, balanced shoulders, or static portrait posing
- Sexualized costume cuts, high heels, or fashion-editorial styling
- Childlike proportions or infantilized behaviour
- Excessive contamination that makes her appear infected or ill
- Literal hospital backgrounds, signage, logos, or readable labels

# AI Generation Notes

Future generated artwork should prioritize the enormous tilted cap as the single signature feature, supported by a compact forward-leaning body and an eager asymmetric face. Preserve her warmth, curiosity, and excessive attentiveness without normalizing her into a believable nurse or pushing her toward horror. The cap must dominate the silhouette but should not hide the eyes. Reject outputs that imitate the Masked Surgeon’s hair, posture, eyewear, or controlled expression. Review the design first in silhouette and grayscale, then assess costume and materials.
