# Visual Direction

## Purpose

This document defines the provisional visual identity of Spread.
It is intended for concept art, AI image generation, asset production,
UI design, animation, and future collaboration with artists.

Nothing here changes the implemented game rules.

## Core visual idea

Spread is an absurd psychological dark-comedy universe built around infection imagery. It is not a believable medical world. Characters are iconic mascots whose professions and roles are inferred through exaggerated visual language rather than realistic depiction.

The visual world should feel:

- psychologically weird
- readable
- darkly humorous
- absurd and unpredictable
- grotesquely caricatured without becoming horrific
- silhouette-driven
- expressive and visually chaotic
- memorable rather than believable
- distinctive rather than conventionally casino-like

The game should avoid:

- generic luxury casino visuals
- gold-and-diamond aesthetics
- realistic hospital photography
- graphic gore
- generic zombie imagery
- copied visual identities from existing slot games
- believable medical characters
- realistic anatomy and ordinary human proportions
- body horror, slasher imagery, and hospital horror

## Design priorities

1. Iconic before realistic
2. Personality before profession
3. Silhouette before details
4. Exaggeration before anatomy
5. Expression before accuracy
6. Memorable before believable
7. Stylization before realism
8. Psychological weirdness before horror
9. Dark humour before violence

## Intended audience impression

The player should immediately understand:

1. every character belongs to the same strange, infected universe;
2. personality is visible before profession;
3. every symbol has an instantly recognizable silhouette;
4. the cast is psychologically unusual but not monstrous;
5. the player should smile before feeling uncomfortable.

## Art-style candidates

The first concept phase should explore:

1. Stylized grotesque caricature
2. Animated psychological dark comedy
3. Surreal mascot design
4. Distorted comic-book illustration
5. Unnatural clay-like character forms

No final style has been selected.

## Shape language

Human symbols:
- exaggerated mascot proportions
- dominant facial features and psychologically unusual expressions
- strongly different silhouettes, posture, and body rhythms
- human identity without realistic anatomy

Equipment symbols:
- simpler shapes
- visually lower value
- recognizable at small slot-symbol size

VirusWild:
- sharp, active, unstable silhouette
- visually dominant
- must look capable of spreading

InfectedWild:
- recognizable relationship to VirusWild
- still shows remnants of the infected character
- visually distinct from the original VirusWild

MaskedSurgeon:
- iconic, protected mascot silhouette
- exaggerated face, unusual hair, and charismatic unsettling smile
- strange posture and unmistakable proportions
- profession suggested rather than literally reproduced

## Symbol list

### MaskedSurgeon

Role:
- highest-value paying symbol
- immune to infection

Visual ideas:
- charismatic, unsettling smile
- oversized facial features and unusual hair
- exaggerated protective shapes
- strange, memorable posture
- unexpected asymmetry

### Nurse

Role:
- valuable vulnerable human

Visual ideas:
- mask incorrectly worn under the chin
- psychologically expressive and chaotically careless
- iconic hairstyle, proportions, and silhouette

### Human2

Role:
- vulnerable human

Visual ideas:
- exaggerated anxiety embodied in face and posture
- memorable mascot proportions
- unmistakably different silhouette from Human3

### Human3

Role:
- vulnerable human

Visual ideas:
- surreal overconfidence or confused intensity
- distorted clothing rhythm and iconic head shape

### Equipment1–3

Role:
- lower-value symbols

Possible concepts:
- syringe
- gloves
- medicine bottle
- thermometer
- damaged protective equipment

Every object should be caricatured through proportion, contamination, wear, or strange personality rather than depicted as a realistic product.

Final objects are not yet selected.

### VirusWild

Role:
- generated Wild
- spreads infection

Visual ideas:
- living virus creature
- unstable glow
- tendrils or directional motion
- dominant silhouette

### InfectedWild

Role:
- transformed vulnerable human
- acts as Wild
- does not spread infection

Visual ideas:
- corrupted version of the original character
- visible infection marks
- less energetic than VirusWild
- no directional spreading effect

## Infection transformation

The transformation must remain readable during animation:

normal human
→ infection contact
→ corrupted intermediate state
→ InfectedWild

The final state should clearly communicate that:

- the character has changed;
- the result is now a Wild;
- it is not the original spreading VirusWild.

## Color direction

Provisional palette roles:

- infection: acidic green or unnatural cyan
- danger: magenta, red, or violet accent
- protected character: muted teal, charcoal, black fabric, and dark green accents
- human characters: deep desaturated colours with contaminated accents
- equipment: oxidized metals, chemical stains, and unnatural textures
- infection: green contamination and strange fluids rather than blood
- background: dark enough for silhouette readability

Darkness should come from colour, material decay, chemical contamination, and psychological unease. Blood, gore, and violence are not the primary visual language.

Exact colors remain open.

## Symbol production requirements

Every symbol should eventually have:

- a square master illustration;
- transparent background;
- readable silhouette at small size;
- consistent camera angle;
- consistent lighting;
- consistent outline treatment;
- normal state where applicable;
- infected state where applicable.

Recommended working master size:

2048 × 2048 px

Final runtime sizes will be exported separately.

## AI generation rules

AI-generated concepts are exploratory source material.

For every accepted concept:

- record the tool and model;
- save the prompt;
- save reference images;
- retain generation date;
- check commercial-use conditions;
- manually edit obvious inconsistencies;
- avoid direct imitation of living artists or existing slot titles.
