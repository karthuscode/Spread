# AI Generation Guide

## Purpose

This guide defines how an image-generation model should interpret every *Spread* Image Specification. It is shared across all symbols and does not replace the symbol-specific specification.

## Source of Truth

Treat the Image Specification as the primary visual source. Translate its approved attributes into concept art faithfully and coherently. Do not redesign the subject, substitute a more familiar archetype, or make the subject more ordinary, anatomically plausible, or professionally realistic.

When this guide and an Image Specification are provided together:

- the Image Specification defines **what** must appear;
- this guide defines **how** to prioritize and interpret it.

Do not require narrative or gameplay documentation to complete the image.

## Interpretation Priorities

Apply the following priorities in order:

1. Iconic mascot identity
2. Unusual and recognizable silhouette
3. Visual personality and psychological weirdness
4. Exaggerated facial design, proportions, and posture
5. Readability at small slot-symbol scale
6. Consistency with the shared dark-comedy universe
7. Costume, material, and colour hierarchy
8. Secondary detail

If detail conflicts with silhouette or readability, simplify the detail. Intentionally exaggerate proportions when this strengthens identity. Prefer memorable caricature over anatomical correctness and expressive distortion over believable realism.

## Fidelity and Invention

Use the specification to resolve the design. Do not invent gameplay, abilities, symbols, text, logos, narrative events, environments, or accessories that are not visually required. Do not add casino luxury cues by default.

Minor connective decisions may be made when necessary to produce a coherent image, such as resolving an unspecified seam or choosing a restrained neutral background. Unspecified anatomy should be stylized rather than normalized. Such decisions must remain subordinate and must not alter identity, personality, silhouette, hierarchy, or intended impression.

Encourage unusual hair, asymmetry, strange posture, unexpected shape relationships, and memorable facial features when supported by the specification. Psychological weirdness should create charisma and unease without turning the subject into a monster.

Do not silently choose among unresolved alternatives. Return ambiguity for human review when it materially changes the design.

## Cross-Symbol Consistency

Artwork created for different symbols should feel part of one visual system. Maintain compatible rendering depth, camera logic, lighting treatment, outline behavior, material simplification, and icon-scale readability. Preserve deliberate hierarchy: not every symbol should receive equal complexity, contrast, or visual authority.

Do not normalize distinctive characters into the same face, body type, costume structure, or pose. Every character must be recognizable as a *Spread* mascot through bold caricature and strange personality, while remaining individually unmistakable. Consistency means a shared universe of psychological dark comedy, not repetition.

## Concept-Art Standard

Generate exploratory professional concept art suitable for human review and later refinement. The output should clearly demonstrate mascot identity, silhouette, exaggerated proportions, facial personality, palette relationships, materials, expression, and composition.

Do not treat the result as a finished production asset. Avoid false polish that hides unresolved forms, excessive micro-detail, or presentation effects that prevent evaluation of the underlying design.

Use a simple transparent or neutral background unless the Image Specification explicitly requires otherwise. Keep the subject clearly framed and unobstructed.

## Prohibited Drift

Avoid:

- redesigning the subject around generic genre conventions;
- inventing gameplay or story content;
- adding unrequested props, characters, typography, or scenery;
- introducing photorealism, believable anatomy, anime, chibi, casino luxury, gore, zombies, body horror, slasher imagery, or hospital horror;
- making a character look like an ordinary real-world professional;
- reducing unusual features in pursuit of correctness or beauty;
- copying the identity of an existing game, franchise, or living artist;
- allowing decorative detail to weaken silhouette or expression;
- concealing important features with effects, reflections, or dramatic cropping;
- treating open questions as permanent design decisions.

## Review Expectation

The generated image is a proposal against the Image Specification. Human reviewers will assess fidelity, clarity, consistency, and useful discoveries. Deviations should be visible and reviewable rather than disguised as completed decisions. Future iterations should respond to documented feedback while preserving all elements that remain approved.
